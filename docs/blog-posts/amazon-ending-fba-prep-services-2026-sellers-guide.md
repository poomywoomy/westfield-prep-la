## üí° 2. Why Amazon Is Making This Dramatic Change

Amazon announced this decision on July 28, 2025, and their reasoning reveals a strategic pivot. According to Amazon, they've observed "a significant improvement in seller packaging capabilities" and reduced demand for prep services as sellers have developed relationships with manufacturers and third-party partners.

**Translation:** Amazon wants to focus on core fulfillment operations rather than value-added services that eat into their margins and warehouse capacity.

The math makes sense from Amazon's perspective:

- Prep services require specialized labor and expensive equipment
- Processing times increase when items need preparation (slowing down customer orders)
- Warehouse space is consumed by prep operations instead of inventory storage
- Quality control becomes more complex with multiple prep requirements

By eliminating these services, Amazon can redirect resources toward faster fulfillment and higher inventory turnover‚Äîthe metrics that directly impact customer satisfaction and their bottom line.

**Our take:** This isn't just about Amazon cutting costs. It's about Amazon pushing operational complexity back to sellers, freeing up fulfillment center capacity for Prime Day and holiday peaks. Sellers who don't adapt quickly will face rejected shipments and lost sales.
